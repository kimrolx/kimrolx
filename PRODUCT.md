# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mixed visitors landing on Kim Roland Berame's personal site: recruiters and hiring managers scanning for fit, engineering peers and leads judging technical depth, and prospective clients or collaborators. Most arrive from a resume link, GitHub, or a referral, often on mobile, skimming. They want to answer "who is this, what can they build, are they worth contacting" in under a minute, then optionally dig into projects and experience.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio for a software engineer (TypeScript/NestJS/PostgreSQL/React full stack, plus CI/CD, Docker, and Azure cloud delivery). It exists to establish credibility fast and convert a skim into a contact or a deeper read of the work. Success: a visitor leaves understanding what Kim builds, trusts the competence, and knows how to reach him or see his code.

## Brand Personality

Precise, confident, refined. Engineer-forward voice that proves competence without bragging, paired with a calm, minimal surface that lets the work speak. No fluff, no hype. The warmth lives in small human touches (the multilingual typewriter greeting, the Philippines accent) against an otherwise restrained, polished frame.

## Anti-references

- **Generic dev-template portfolio**: the cookie-cutter hero + skill-icon grid + endless identical project cards every bootcamp grad ships. Avoid the template smell.
- **Corporate SaaS landing**: sterile, buzzword-heavy, stock-photo enterprise tone. This is a person, not a product page.
- **Cluttered / dense**: no wall of content, no cramped sections. Breathing room is part of the credibility.

## Design Principles

- **Show, don't tell.** Real projects, real stack, real links over adjectives. The work is the argument.
- **Skim-first, depth-on-demand.** The one-minute scan must land; detail rewards those who stay.
- **Restraint reads as confidence.** Minimal surface, deliberate motion. Quiet beats loud here.
- **Practice what you preach.** A site by an engineer who values craft must itself be fast, accessible, and bug-free.
- **Human in the details.** Personality shows through small, earned touches, not theme-wide noise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1, semantic markup, keyboard-navigable, against the near-black achromatic field. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` everywhere: Lenis smooth scroll yields to native scrolling, the hero load stagger and scroll reveals resolve to fully visible, and the typewriter greeting holds on one language. Motion is restrained by default (a load stagger, scroll reveals, one thin scroll rule), so there is no scroll-jacking to disable.
