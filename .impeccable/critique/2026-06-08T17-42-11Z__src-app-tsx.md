## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Not obvious AI slop. The dark "Midnight Workshop" system is committed and disciplined — signal-color restraint, tonal depth over shadows, two-font rule, single-flag gradient, reveals that resolve to visible. But the *hero stacks the genre's four most template-adjacent moves at once*: multilingual typewriter greeting, rotating job title, animate-bounce scroll arrow, drifting glow orbs. Individually fine; together they read closer to "well-executed familiar dev portfolio" than "how was this made?". The execution clears the generic-template bar; the idea vocabulary doesn't quite.

**Deterministic scan (`detect.mjs src`):** 2 warnings, both `Hero.tsx`.
- `gradient-text` @ Hero.tsx:23 — the Philippines flag gradient. Sanctioned by the design system (The One Flag Rule), so not slop, BUT the dark-blue end (`#0038A8`) on near-black `#0a0a0a` is near-invisible (~1.7:1). Legibility problem regardless of intent.
- `bounce-easing` @ Hero.tsx:45 — `animate-bounce` on the scroll arrow. A real tell per the no-bounce rule.

**Visual overlays:** No browser automation available in this environment; no user-visible overlay was produced. Findings below are from source + contrast math, not a rendered capture.

## Overall Impression
This is a genuinely good, shippable portfolio with real craft and accessibility discipline — well above the bootcamp-template floor it explicitly rejects. Biggest opportunity: it under-sells the engineer. The credibility goal (convert a recruiter skim into contact) is undercut by thin/duplicative proof, no resume, fabricated demo metrics, and a few low-contrast text roles.

## What's Working
- **Committed dark system, not a toggle.** Glow-orb ambient light, tonal surface steps, hairline structure, ≤10% signal color. Coherent voice.
- **Accessibility scaffolding is real.** Skip link, sr-only greeting, aria-labels, focus-visible amber ring, prefers-reduced-motion across orbs/typewriter/reveals, reveals visible-by-default (no blank-section trap).
- **Signal-not-fill discipline.** Tech tags are neutral chips with a color dot; status as a colored dot+word. Restraint reads as confidence, as intended.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Fabricated metrics on a credibility-first page.** Hero's terminal fallback hardcodes `2,481 clicks · 99.98% uptime` and Trimly's path — invented numbers rendered as if real, on the one project (Trimly) that has no screenshot. A "show, don't tell" engineer site inventing stats is a trust risk. Fix: ship a real Trimly screenshot, or strip the numbers to an honest mock.
- Suggested command: `/impeccable harden`

**[P1] `ink-faint` (#6b6e74) fails AA for text.** ~3.8:1 on `#0a0a0a` — below 4.5:1. Used for project periods, experience dates, footer, terminal text. DESIGN.md names ink-dim as the floor, but ink-faint is doing body-text duty. Fix: lift ink-faint to ≥4.5:1 or restrict it to non-text only.
- Suggested command: `/impeccable audit`

**[P1] Proof is thin for the stated goal.** 1 experience entry; 3 project cards where 2 are the same EcoPoints build split into mobile+web (padding that reads as identical-card repetition). No resume/CV download, no LinkedIn — both expected by skimming recruiters, who are the named primary audience. Fix: add CV download + LinkedIn to contact/hero; consider merging the two EcoPoints cards.
- Suggested command: `/impeccable layout`

**[P2] Flag gradient legibility + status duplication.** The blue half of "Philippines" disappears on black; and Trimly shows `status:"Production"` pill beside `period:"Production"` — the same word twice. Fix: shift the flag gradient lighter/warmer or set it on a lighter weight, and give Trimly a real period (e.g. "2024 – Present").
- Suggested command: `/impeccable polish`

**[P2] Genre-trope stack + low-info motion.** Typewriter + rotating title + bounce arrow + orbs is the template cluster. The rotating title cross-fades between "Software Engineer" and "Fullstack Engineer" — near-synonyms, so the motion flickers without adding information, on the exact line a recruiter reads first. Fix: drop bounce easing (use ease-out), and either give the rotation distinct roles or make it a single confident title.
- Suggested command: `/impeccable animate`

## Persona Red Flags

**Morgan (Skimming Recruiter — project-specific):** Lands from a resume link on mobile, ~60s. No CV/resume to download, no LinkedIn. The one line they read first (the title) wastes its motion swapping two synonyms. Work section looks light (1 job, effectively 2 distinct projects). Fabricated uptime stat, if noticed, reads as padding.

**Riley (Stress Tester):** The terminal "metrics" are invented and the fallback is hardcoded to Trimly — any other image-less project would render Trimly's path/name. `period:"Production"` next to the Production pill is a visible data smell.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile):** Hero is `min-h-svh` with ~7 stacked centered blocks — a lot of vertical scroll before any proof. Nav links are `text-xs` inline targets inside a 56px bar; tap targets are likely under 44px tall. Reduced motion and lazy images are handled well.

## Minor Observations
- `RotatingTitle` changes text with no `aria-live`; screen readers get whatever is current at focus (low concern since it's decorative).
- Typewriter at 230ms/char feels sluggish for longer greetings.
- Skills "Tooling: Git, GitHub" is redundant with GitHub Actions already under DevOps.
- Hero social icons are icon-only (aria-label present, so a11y ok, but visually unlabeled).

## Questions to Consider
- What does a recruiter need in the first 60 seconds that isn't here — and is it one tap away?
- Is the rotating title earning its place, or is one committed title stronger?
- Could a real Trimly screenshot replace both the fake metrics and the gradient legibility risk in one move?
